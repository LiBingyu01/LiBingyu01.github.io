Profile

Biography

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), supervised by Prof. Xuelong Li.

My research focuses on open-world complex scene perception, world action models, and generative video models. I am interested in building systems that can perceive, associate, reason about, and generate dynamic scenes under realistic, complex, and open-ended conditions.

Research

Research Interests

My current work connects open-world perception, temporal association, action-conditioned world representation, and dynamic visual generation.

World Action Models

Action-conditioned world modeling for understanding interactions, dynamics, spatial memory, and future outcomes.

Generative Video Models

Efficient video generation and simulation models for dynamic scene understanding and controllable synthesis.

Open-World Complex Scene Perception

Open-vocabulary perception, segmentation, counting, multimodal fusion, and temporal referring understanding.
