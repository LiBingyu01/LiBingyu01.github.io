01 / PROFILE

About

Research identity organized around one core scientific problem.

My current research centers on open semantic object state modeling. In open environments, a target is not just a category label or a mask; it is a continuously changing state that may include identity, language description, spatial region, quantity relation, visibility, motion, and future tendency.

I organize this direction around three connected questions: how open semantic target states are recognized and initialized from vision-language observations, how they are maintained under dynamic observation conditions, and how they evolve when an intelligent agent acts and changes its observation path.

Recognize and initialize structured target states, maintain identity-consistent states through time, and evolve future states under action.
State InitializationVision-language recognition and state initialization for open categories, descriptions, regions, counts, and relations.
State MaintenanceTemporal consistency under viewpoint change, occlusion, motion, and state variation.

02 · Open Semantic Target State Maintenance

Temporal consistency

This line studies how target identity and state variables can be preserved and updated under temporal change, viewpoint variation, occlusion, visibility shifts, and motion.

An Open-Source Benchmark and Baseline for Multi-temporal Referring Segmentation arXiv 2026
Bingyu Li*, Da Zhang*, Tao Huo, Zhiyuan Zhao, Junyu Gao, Xuelong Li
Paper Project Page Code Dataset Pretraining Dataset Pretrained Model Weights Other LVLMs

MTRefSeg introduces Multi-temporal Referring Segmentation (MTRS), where models compare temporally related images and segment the language-described changed region. The work builds MTRefSeg-21K with 21K bi-image-text-mask annotations and proposes MTRefSeg-R1, a change-aware LVLM trained by vision-only temporal pretraining followed by referring multi-temporal fine-tuning.
